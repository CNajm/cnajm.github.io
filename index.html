  • Renée

    Renée

    I created Renée with a friend out of an idea we both saw potential in. Renée is an autonomous content curation backend designed to identify and upload high quality video clips to a YouTube channel. The clips go through a series of deterministic analysis algorithms and are ranked by a scoring function. I also built a web-based control panel to facilitate usage and diagnostics.

    Renée is closed source for commercial reasons, contact me if you're interested in accessing source code.

  • Applying Computer Vision in Video Games

    Applying Computer Vision in Video Games

    I fused my interests in gaming and programming by applying computer vision techniques to some of the video games I play.


    Albion Online

    I used the scikit-image toolkit to post-process automated screengrabs of the Albion Online auction house and pass them into a trained TesseractOCR engine to extract price and meta data.


    Rust

    Rust is a game with a grid-based inventory system. I extracted the item icons from the game files and used template matching to scan container inventories and build an index of stored items. I then used this as a baseline to develop functionality such as automating inventory sorting and removing of junk items. I served the collected data through a simple REST api in order to access it through a chatbot.
